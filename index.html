mercury

Mercury, the smallest planet (2,439 km radius), orbits the Sun at 36 million miles (0.39 AU) and completes an orbit in 88 days. Its thin atmosphere has oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen. Mercury experiences extreme temperatures, from 800°F (430°C) to -290°F (-180°C). Despite its proximity to the Sun, it’s not the hottest planet; Venus holds that title.

Mars

Mars was known since ancient times and observed by Galileo in 1610. It has a radius of 3,390 km and a thin carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere. Located 142 million miles (1.5 AU) from the Sun, sunlight takes 13 minutes to reach it. Mars is 54.6 million km to 401 million km from Earth. Known as the "Red Planet," it has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, and is the most explored planet with rovers.

Venus

Venus was also observed by Galileo in 1610 and has a radius of 6,052 km. Its thick atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide, causes extreme heat (900°F/475°C). Venus is 67 million miles (0.72 AU) from the Sun, with sunlight reaching it in 6 minutes. It rotates in reverse, with a day lasting 243 Earth days. Venus is the hottest planet, with surface pressure 92 times Earth's.

Top Observation

Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level. It is located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China (Tibet). Known for its challenging climbing conditions, Everest attracts mountaineers from around the globe.

California

California is a state located on the west coast of the United States, known for its diverse geography, including beaches, mountains, and deserts. It is the most populous state and the third-largest by area. Major cities include Los Angeles, San Francisco, and San Diego. California is a hub for technology, entertainment, agriculture, and tourism.

Maldives

The Maldives is a tropical island nation in the Indian Ocean, consisting of 26 coral atolls and over 1,000 islands. Known for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and vibrant marine life, it is a popular tourist destination. The Maldives is renowned for its luxury resorts and is a key location for snorkeling and diving.
